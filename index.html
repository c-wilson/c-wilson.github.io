It is an open secret that defense contract awards are a source of influence within the US political system. Large defense contractors place manufacturing and research facilities in multiple areas of the country so that they can influence the economies of multiple congressional districts and states. It is rumored that members of congress are sometimes willing to support defense spending that will benefit their local economies regardless of the merit of the contracts.


I propose to use Department of Defense (DoD) contract data to map the beneficiaries of this spending over time.



Datasets

Data from contracts is available on the US Department of Defense contracts website.


I wrote a quick and dirty web scraper to scrape contract data from the The idea is to geocode this data, and follow it over time. The dataset sits at just over 300 MB in size. Over 70,000 contracts are included in the dataset.

The plan:

Develop a robust scraper for DoD contract data.

  • Current beta allows for extraction of the date, amount, city, state, and corporation for each award. Geocoding this (extracting lat/long) is implemented with GoogleMaps API.
  • Data is noisy, so there is some refinement necessary.
  • Exploring NLP concept modeling to extract the gist of each contract would be great.

Explore how contract information correlates with the political climate within the US.

Example map of contracts


  • Decompose spatial and temporal correlations in the dataset using matrix factorization techniques. (NMF, PCA)
  • Relate these trends to other datasources such as the Ideology Score for congress members.

Make a predictor for where contract award locations.

  • Does a state’s representation change the frequency of award? Here is an example legislator composition and award frequency over time
  • Do polling data and and the closeness of elections predict the award locations?
  • It would be interesting to do concept modeling of the contract information (ie, weapons vs healthcare, intelligence, research, etc.)
